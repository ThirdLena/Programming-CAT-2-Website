About

YOASOBI is a Japanese pop duo formed in 2019. It consists of musician and record producer Ayase and singer-songwriter Lilas Ikuta who goes under the stage name Ikura

Their Band name comes from the japanese word "夜遊び" Which means "Nightlife". Ayase chose this name to symbolise how they wanted their normal daily life to be seperated from their music which they mainly made at night

They represent themselves with the slogan "novel into music"(小説を音楽にするユニット) Therefore, if their song is not based on a fictional story, they are not credited under Yoasobi but under their indiviual names.

Before forming the band, both of the members had active careers in music. Ayase was the Vocalist of Davinci, a rock band. It was active from 2012-2018 and eventually disbanded in 2020

Lilas Ikuta, who later adopted the stage Ikura, was a singer-songwriter of the cover group Plusonica from 2017 to 2021



Artistry

Yoasobi's musical style has been mainly described as a mix of pop, rock, electronic, During an interview with Associated press Ayase explained their music genre: "We think that Yoasobi is its own genre. There's no specific genre they're going after we're going after. We're definitely influenced by J-pop in general.

In their childhood, Ayase was influenced by '80s and '90s music, K-pop, J-pop, and folk music. While Ikura enjoyed Western music such as songs from Disney, folk, and country music, as well as Japanese R&B and hip-hop.

Ayase interprets and writes Yoasobi's songs reflecting on his feelings and experiences based on the story. While Ikura sings them on the perspective of the protagonist or narrator of the story, not from herself.



History

2019-2021: Formation, 夜に駆ける and THE BOOK:

In 2019, Yohei Yashiro, a founder of Monogatary.com, presented the idea of project to produce songs inspired by selected story submissions published on the website as a prize to authors instead of book or anime adaptation, which not all were successful.

Later, Yashiro enlisted Shuya yamamoto, who handled SMEJ's artists at the time. In the mid-year, Ayase recieved an offer from Yashiro and Yamamoto to create songs for the project. During a disscusion about vocalists, Ayase found ikura from cover of Aimyon's "君はロックを聴かない"(Kimi wa Rock o Kikanai). He later checked her Youtube Channel, where she posted some of her covers, and contected her to persuade her to form the duo. Yoasobi announced the debut teaser on October 1, which they marked as their anniversary.

The first song released by Yoasobi was "夜に駆ける" (Into The Night), which was based on the short story "タナトスの誘惑" (Thanatos no Yūwaku) written by Mayo Hoshino, it won the novel contest Monocon 2019. It was the First song in japan for the RIAJ to certify the song Diamond (500M Streams), and double diamond (1B Streams).

Some other notable songs from THE BOOK Include "群青" (Ultramarine), "たぶん" (Tabun) and "あの夢をなぞって" (Tracing A Dream). These songs were created between 2019 - 2021



2021: The Book 2 and English-language releases

First announced in late 2020, Yoasobi recorded both the opening and ending themes for the second season of the Japanese anime series Beastars. Its opening theme, "怪物" (Monster), was released on January 6, 2021, It was the same date as they realeased their EP The BOOK. Its ending theme, "優しい彗星" (Comet), came on the 20th.

Yoasobi's released their second EP, The Book 2, on December 1, 2021. The EP features the duo's all singles released in that year, such as "怪物" (Monster), "もしも命が描けたら" (If I Could Draw Life) and "優しい彗星" (Comet)

Furthermore, YOASOBI began to explore English Language songs by translating the japanese songs they already made. These songs made up their first English EP "E-side".



2022-2023: Hajimete no, "Idol" and The Book 3

Yoasobi released their first video album, The Film, on March 23, containing videos of the duo's three concerts held in 2021 and unreleased footage from their episode on the documentary program JMnetsu Tairiku.

They also created the first opening theme of the mecha anime series "Mobile Suit Gundam: The Witch from Mercury", it was titled "祝福" (The Blessing)

YOASOBI Released their EP E-side 2 on november 18th. They also did their first overseas performance in December 2022 at the Head in the Clouds Festival in Indonesia and the Philippines.

In 2023, Yoasobi was in charge of the opening theme of the anime series Oshi no Ko, titled "アイドル" (Idol), based on manga artist Aka Akasaka's short story 45510. It was the fastest song for the RIAJ to certify it diamond within 295 days since its release. Globally "Idol" peaked 7th on the Billboard Global 200, the highest position achieved by a japanese act.

Yoasobi released their third EP, The Book 3, on October 4, composed of all singles released from 2022 to the September 2023, which included the first opening theme for the anime series Frieren: Beyond Journey's End, titled "勇者" (The Brave).
